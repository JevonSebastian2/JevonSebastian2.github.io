About Sirclo

Sirclo is an Indonesian commerce enabler that helps brands and SMEs sell across multiple online channels from a single platform. Sellers connect their stores to Tokopedia, Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop, and others through one unified dashboard.

In Q4 2023, a marketing push drove a spike in new seller registrations. The growth team expected a corresponding lift in activated sellers. Instead, activation numbers stayed flat. The problem was in onboarding.

Solution

A guided, 3-phase delivery plan

As Project Manager, I scoped the solution into three deliverable phases, each with its own sprint, clear acceptance criteria, and usability test cycle. This let us validate early and adjust before committing to full build.

MVP 1
First Impression — "The Great Getting Started"
Added a push notification trigger post-download. Redesigned the authentication page to present only 2 choices (Sign Up / Sign In) instead of 8. Introduced a business-type selector so the platform could personalise the experience from the first step. Sprint duration: 2 weeks.
MVP 2
Guided Dashboard — "The Beginning of Comfort"
Replaced the raw dashboard with a guided onboarding checklist for first-time sellers. Two tasks shown: "Add First Product" and "Make First Transaction." Progress bar updates in real time. Returning sellers see a personalised welcome and quick-access shortcuts. Sprint duration: 2 weeks.
MVP 3
Transaction Confidence — "Eternal Love"
Redesigned the transaction flow end-to-end. Product catalog, item counter, order summary, and payment selection consolidated into 4 screens (down from 8). Promo visibility added inline. Contextual tooltips added for first-time users at every decision point. Sprint duration: 2 weeks.
Before & After

What changed

Before

  • 8+ choices presented on the authentication page
  • No onboarding checklist or progress state

  • Error messages were generic and unhelpful

After
  • Guided checklist shown on first login with 2 clear next steps
  • Authentication reduced to 2 primary actions
  • Progress bar tracks completion in real time
  • First transaction completable in 4 steps
  • Error messages rewritten to be specific and recovery-focused
  • Business-type selector personalises the dashboard from step one
  • 52% reduction in drop-off, 2.1x activation rate
Project Management

How I ran the project

  • Defined the full project scope, timeline, and sprint structure across 3 two-week cycles in week 1
  • Wrote the Project Requirements Document (PRD) covering problem statement, goals, user stories, acceptance criteria, and out-of-scope items
  • Facilitated two design sprints with the UI/UX team to generate and evaluate solution concepts before committing to build
  • Built and maintained the product backlog in Jira, prioritised with MoSCoW framework, and ran daily standups throughout each sprint
  • Coordinated dependencies between design, frontend, and backend teams to prevent blockers from delaying sprint deliverables
  • Organised usability testing with 6 real sellers per MVP phase, synthesised findings into iteration briefs for the next sprint
  • Reported sprint progress and key metrics to stakeholders in weekly reviews, adjusting scope when necessary to protect the launch date

Learnings

What I took away

Define acceptance criteria before writing a single line of code
Every feature we built had a clear, testable acceptance criterion agreed upon before the sprint started. This eliminated ambiguity, reduced rework, and gave engineering a shared definition of done.
Scope creep is a project manager's primary enemy
Stakeholders repeatedly requested new features mid-sprint. I created a formal change request process with a one-week delay rule. This kept each MVP on schedule while still capturing good ideas for the next phase.
Ship in phases, validate fast, adjust accordingly
Breaking the redesign into three MVPs let us validate assumptions with real sellers before building the full system. MVP 1 findings directly reshaped the scope of MVP 2, saving two full weeks of build time.
